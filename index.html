Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about InvisiCrypt and steganography.

Is this tool really secure?

Yes. This tool uses AES-256-GCM, which is an industry-standard, authenticated encryption algorithm. This means your data is not only encrypted, but also protected from tampering. The decryption will fail if the password is wrong or the hidden data is modified by even one bit.

Is my data uploaded to your server?

Absolutely not. All processing (embedding, extracting, analysis) happens 100% locally in your web browser. Your files and your password never leave your computer. This is a key privacy and security feature of InvisiCrypt. You can disconnect from the internet after loading the page, and the tool will continue to work perfectly.

Why does "Analyze" flag my stego image?

Because it's working correctly! The "Detect" tab runs a Chi-Square statistical test. A normal, "clean" image has natural patterns, which results in a LOW Chi-Square score. Encrypted data is perfectly random. Embedding this random data *destroys* the image's natural patterns, resulting in a HIGH Chi-Square score, which the tool flags as "Detected."

Why does embedding fail on some files?

The most common reason is capacity. Your secret message (after compression and encryption) is simply too large to fit in the carrier file you chose. You can try to fix this by:
1. Choosing a larger image or audio file (more pixels/samples = more space).
2. Choosing a higher bit depth (e.g., 2-bit or 3-bit) to use more space per pixel, though this is slightly less secure.
3. Ensuring "Compress Message" is checked.

Why only WAV and not MP3?

WAV and PNG are "lossless" formats, meaning they store raw, uncompressed data. This makes it easy to modify the least-significant bits (LSBs) without corrupting the file. MP3 and JPEG are "lossy" compressed formats. They do not store raw samples/pixels, and changing even one bit can corrupt the entire file. Hiding data in MP3s is a completely different, far more complex process that is not based on LSB.
